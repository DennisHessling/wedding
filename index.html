A suburban train goes to Pomáz from Margit híd Budai hídfő (Margaret-bridge, buda side). There is an extra fee, 250 HUF/direction because Pomáz is not a district of BP. You can simple purchase this ticket on the train when the controller comes. The suburban train is signed with a H on the website.
The Budapest Card is a bit more expensive but entrance fees for a number of museums etc. are included. Website for planning your trip with public transport: futar.bkk.hu/?layers=svb&lang=en Google maps also works rather well for public transport.

From the airport there is the bus 200E which goes to Kőbánya-Kispest Metro station. From there you can take the metro which has multiple stops in the city center. Timetable
There is a bus 100E which only has a few stops until the center. You need to buy a special ticket for this bus for 900 HUF (3 euro).Timetable

Money

Hungary has Hungarian forints, written HUF, Huf, or Ft.
1 Euro = 319 Huf (24.05.2018)
Calculator
FreeCurrencyRates.com
To get Hungarian forint, you can simply pick up some at any ATM in Hungary. It will ask you about the currency in which you want to deduct it from your bank account. Pick forint to have your home bank choose a conversion rate for you, not the bank that put an ATM up at the airport.
